LADAKH



Ladakh, often called the "Land of High Passes," is a mountainous region in northern India, known for its stunning landscapes, Buddhist monasteries, and unique culture. It's characterized by high-altitude deserts, snow-capped mountains, and deep river valleys. Leh is the main city and a popular base for exploring the region.

Ladakh, also known as the "Land of High Passes", is special due to its unique high-altitude cold desert landscape, ancient monasteries, and vibrant Buddhist culture. It's renowned for its stunning natural beauty, including breathtaking mountain ranges, crystal-clear lakes like Pangong Tso and Tso Moriri, and the highest saltwater lake in the world, Pangong Tso.
